# Jennifer's Book Analysis

A structured repository for the literary analysis and editorial development of _Dream House_ — a psychological thriller by Jennifer. This serves as the central source of truth for all structural analysis, critical commentary, editorial notes, and phase deliverables accumulated throughout the project.

---

## Project Overview

This repository supports a professional editorial and critical analysis workflow conducted from the perspective of a professional reader with a background in classical literature, drama, and critical theory.

The analysis is rigorous and unsparing. The standard applied throughout is not "is this good enough" but "is this as strong as it can be." We define what the book is trying to do before judging how well it does it — and we hold it to the full weight of the tragic architecture it has chosen.

The work has established that this is not a psychological thriller with borrowed tragic elements. It is a Greek tragedy that uses a psychological thriller as its dramatic mechanism. That distinction governs every analytical decision in this repository.

---

## The Book in One Sentence

A woman haunted by a recurring nightmare she believes is a premonition investigates her neighbor for a series of murders — and discovers that the killer is her half-brother, the living consequence of her father's buried sin, and the void she has spent her life trying to fill.

## Analytical Framework

The analysis is organized into five sequential phases, each with defined scope and deliverables. Phases must be completed in order.

| Phase       | Focus                                                         | Primary Frameworks                                              |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Phase 1** | Deconstruction — what is here                                 | Premise, spine map, want/need audit, value inventory, world map |
| **Phase 2** | Structural Diagnosis — does the structure hold                | Aristotle, Freytag                                              |
| **Phase 3** | Dramatic Mechanics — does each scene work                     | McKee, Frank Daniel Sequence Method                             |
| **Phase 4** | Thematic & Premise Audit — does the book know what it's about | Egri, Truby                                                     |
| **Phase 5** | Synthesis — what does the author need to do                   | Cross-lane findings, priority ranking, editorial recommendation |

Full framework details are documented in `phase-architecture.md`.

## Methodology

The analytical framework applied here prioritizes:

- **Classical literary standards** — structure, unity, and the traditions of dramatic and narrative form
- **Tragic architecture** — this book is held to the standard of _anagnorisis_, _peripeteia_, and _hamartia_ as load-bearing structural requirements, not decorative influences
- **Critical theory** — close reading, textual analysis, and contextual interpretation
- **Editorial rigor** — honest, high-standard critique aimed at strengthening the work, not protecting it
- **Source transparency** — all claims, comparisons, and references are documented and traceable within this repo
